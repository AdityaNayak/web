Success of an org depends on its:

  1. Decision Velocity
  2. Decision Accuracy
  3. Decision Bandwidth

Increasing decentralization of decision-making as much as possible without it compromising the integrity of output; is a possible strategy.

DAOs are nowhere close.

Not decentralized

If the purpose of an organization is value maximization; that requires accurate decision-making at every level.

This can be visualized as a decision tree:

Decentralized decision making would mean that the node at depth 4 is able to make the right decisions on its own.

Instead, in DAOs that decision load will travel all the way to depth 1 (aka centralization), where a general consensus will solve it.

However, they are not enough. The problem is much more core. The problem is:

Democracy

Airdrops, liquidity mining, and every other token ‘giving’ idea creates a class of entitled mobs that don’t have much to offer but noise.

Decision making is not an entitlement. It’s a reward for good judgement. Treating it otherwise creates mobs. Mobs don’t create value.

Democracies create public goods. Public goods create a disincentive for risk-taking and breed politics.

Politics shift the winning strategy from truth seeking (and enterprise) to narrative building.

In democracies, the loudest voice wins; not the truest.

Individuals search for truth, groups search for consensus.
